How GST Billing Works for Restaurants in India

February 15, 2026 ¬∑ 8 min read ¬∑ PeeledOnion Team

If you run a restaurant, cafe, bakery, or cloud kitchen in India, understanding GST (Goods and Services Tax) billing is essential. The GST system replaced multiple indirect taxes in 2017, and all food-service businesses are required to comply with its invoicing rules.

In this guide, we explain the GST rates that apply to restaurants, what a GST-compliant invoice looks like, and how you can use free billing software like PeeledOnion to generate proper invoices automatically.

What Is GST and Why Does It Matter for Restaurants?

GST is a unified indirect tax levied on goods and services across India. For restaurants, it replaced the earlier combination of VAT, service tax, and other levies with a single tax structure.

Every restaurant that earns above the GST registration threshold must charge GST on its bills and file periodic returns with the government. Even if you are below the threshold, registering voluntarily allows you to issue GST-compliant invoices and claim input tax credits.

GST Rates for Restaurants in India

The GST rate a restaurant charges depends on its type and whether it claims input tax credit (ITC):

  • 5% GST (without ITC): Most restaurants, including small and mid-sized dine-in restaurants, takeaway joints, cafes, and cloud kitchens. This is the most common rate.
  • 18% GST (with ITC): Restaurants located inside hotels with room tariffs above ‚Çπ7,500 per night.
  • 5% GST on food delivery: Food aggregators like Swiggy and Zomato collect and deposit GST at 5% on delivery orders.

For most independent restaurants, cafes, and bakeries, the applicable rate is 5% GST without input tax credit.

CGST and SGST: How the Tax Is Split

GST is divided into two components for intra-state transactions (when the restaurant and customer are in the same state):

  • CGST (Central GST): Goes to the central government.
  • SGST (State GST): Goes to the state government.

For a 5% GST rate, your invoice will show 2.5% CGST and 2.5% SGST. For inter-state transactions, the entire amount is charged as IGST (Integrated GST).

What a GST-Compliant Restaurant Invoice Must Include

A valid GST invoice from a restaurant must contain the following details:

  1. Restaurant name and address
  2. GSTIN (GST Identification Number)
  3. Invoice number (unique and sequential)
  4. Date of issue
  5. Customer name and address (for B2B transactions)
  6. Description of items (food and beverages served)
  7. HSN/SAC codes for the items
  8. Quantity and value of each item
  9. GST rate and amount (CGST + SGST or IGST)
  10. Total amount including tax

For B2C (business-to-consumer) transactions under ‚Çπ200, a simplified bill of supply can be issued instead.

Common GST Billing Mistakes Restaurants Make

  • Wrong GST rate: Using 18% when 5% applies (or vice versa).
  • Missing GSTIN: Not printing your restaurant's GSTIN on the invoice.
  • Incorrect tax split: Showing only "GST 5%" instead of splitting into CGST 2.5% and SGST 2.5%.
  • No invoice number: Issuing bills without unique sequential numbers.
  • No digital records: Relying on paper bills that can be lost or damaged.
